DIY PS5 Trigger Fix▏

This project showcases how to (temporarily) fix your PlayStation 5 (DualSense) controller's trigger piston mechanism.

WARNING: Will void warranty (if applicable). Different chassis and revisions may vary.

Images provided in this guide are sourced from a donor controller, same principle applies.

Tools Required

  • Precision Screwdriver set, preferably magnetic-tipped, with a PH0 size head.
  • Small prying tool.
  • A decent amount of time and patience (precision mounts, thin cables, small gears).

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Carefully remove L1 and R1 bumper (shoulder) buttons to reveal the concealed PH0 x 3mm screws.
  2. Carefully remove the front shell (faceplate). WARNING: This piece is brittle, and likely to crack or snap.

    3. Feel free to clean the controller at this step. Lotta gunk gets onto the built-in speaker.

  3. This should reveal two more PH0 x 3mm screws under the "feet" of the controller. Remove those, too.
  4. Carefully remove the Back Shell (Rear Housing) from the Inner Frame (Midframe).

    5. At this step, it is a good idea to disconnect the battery. Regardless, you will have to remove the plastic holding the battery in place.

    Please disconnect the battery, unlike in this image. Also, be careful with the front-facing microphone.

  5. Find and remove the plastic housing covering the gear mechanism for the faulty trigger.
  6. Carefully invert the spring. It may have already popped out. Put it in, and try the trigger. If it feels soft, invert it, and try again. Whichever side has the most traction is the one you want to keep.
  7. Reassemble the controller.

    8. If you somehow have two left over screws after reassembly, but your controller now works fine, congratulations, you have reached Modders Delight level of modding.
